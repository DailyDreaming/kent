Description

This track shows genes targeted by Knockout Mouse Project (KOMP), a trans-NIH initiative to generate a public resource of mouse embryonic stem (ES) cells containing a null mutation in every gene in the mouse genome. Gene targets are color-coded by status:

  • Green: Reagent(s) Available
  • Yellow: In Progress
  • Blue: Not Started/On Hold
  • Black: Withdrawn/Problematic

The KOMP Data Coordination Center (KOMP DCC) is the central database resource for coordinating mouse gene targeting within KOMP, serving as the central public interface for the KOMP and providing web-based query and display tools for KOMP data. In addition, the website provides a tool for the scientific community to nominate genes of interest to be knocked-out by the KOMP. KOMP includes two production centers:

  • CSD, a collaborative team at the Children's Hospital Oakland Research Institute (CHORI), the Wellcome Trust Sanger Institute and the University of California at Davis School of Veterinary Medicine.
  • a team at the VelociGene division of Regeneron Pharmaceuticals, Inc.

Methods

Using complementary targeting strategies, the two production centers design and create targeting vectors, mutant ES cell lines and, to some extent, mutant mice, embryos or sperm and transfer these materials to the KOMP Repository. The KOMP Repository archives, maintains, and distributes KOMP products. Researchers can order products and get product information from the Repository. Researchers can also express interest in products that are still in the pipeline. They will then receive email notification as soon as these KOMP products are available for distribution.
