Description

The UCSC Known Genes track shows known protein-coding genes based on protein data from UniProt (SWISS-PROT and TrEMBL) and mRNA data from the NCBI reference sequences collection (RefSeq) and GenBank.

Display Conventions and Configuration

This track follows the display conventions for gene prediction tracks. Black coloring indicates features that have corresponding entries in the Protein Databank (PDB). Blue indicates features associated with mRNAs from NCBI RefSeq or (dark blue) items having associated proteins in the SWISS-PROT database. The variation in blue shading of RefSeq items corresponds to the level of review the RefSeq record has undergone: predicted (light), provisional (medium), or reviewed (dark).

This track contains an optional codon coloring feature that allows users to quickly validate and compare gene predictions. To display codon colors, select the genomic codons option from the Color track by codons pull-down menu. Click here for more information about this feature.

Methods

mRNA sequences from RefSeq and GenBank were aligned against the $organism genome using blat. When a single mRNA aligned in multiple places, only alignments having at least 98% base identity with the genomic sequence were kept. The mouse protein sequences were also aligned.

The genomic mRNA and protein alignments were compared, and protein-mRNA pairings were determined from alignments with significant overlap. CDS data for the mRNAs were obtained from RefSeq, GenBank and UCSC protein-mRNA blat alignments. The initial set of UCSC Known Genes candidates, comprised of the mRNA alignments with their CDS data, was then analyzed by a gene check program to remove questionable candidates, such as those with in-frame stop codons, missing start or stop codons, etc.

Among multiple mRNAs referenced by a single protein, the best mRNA was chosen, based on a quality score derived from its length, the level of the match between its translation and the protein sequence, and its release date. The resulting mRNA and protein pairs were further consolidated by removing duplicated entries with identical CDS regions to produce the final data set shown in this track.
