The Archaeoglobus fulgidus DSM4304 genome sequence is 2,178,400 bp long and contains approximately 2,420 protein-coding genes.  A. fulgidus is a sulfur-metabolizing anaerobe that can grow at extremely high temperatures. High-temperature sulfate reduction by Archaeoglobus species contributes to deep subsurface oil-well 'souring' by iron sulfide, which causes corrosion of iron and steel in oil-and gas-processing systems.

The genome was completed in December 1997 by the Institute for Genomic Research, and was published in

The complete genome sequence of the hyperthermophilic, sulphate-reducing archaeon Archaeoglobus fulgidus.  Klenk HP, Clayton RA, Tomb JF, White O, Nelson KE, Ketchum KA, Dodson RJ, Gwinn M, Hickey EK, Peterson JD, Richardson DL, Kerlavage AR, Graham DE, Kyrpides NC, Fleischmann RD, Quackenbush J, Lee NH, Sutton GG, Gill S, Kirkness EF, Dougherty BA, McKenney K, Adams MD, Loftus B, Peterson S, Reich CI, McNeil LK, Badger JH, Glodek A, Zhou L, Overbeek R, Gocayne JD, Weidman JF, McDonald L, Utterback T, Cotton MD, Spriggs T, Artiach P, Kaine BP, Sykes SM, Sadow PW, D'Andrea KP, Bowman C, Fujii C, Garland SA, Mason TM, Olsen GJ, Fraser CM, Smith HO, Woese CR, Venter JC.  Nature 1997; 390(6658):364-70.

Sample position queries

A genome position can be specified by chromosomal coordinate range, COG ID, or keywords from the GenBank or TIGR description of a gene. The following list shows examples of valid position queries. 

Request:
    Genome Browser Response:

chr
Displays all of chromosome (the entire genome).
chr:1-10000
Displays first ten thousand bases on the chromosome.
NADH
Lists genes with "NADH" in the description.
AF1217
Display chromosome at position of gene AF1217.
AF121
Lists genes with names beginning with "AF121".
