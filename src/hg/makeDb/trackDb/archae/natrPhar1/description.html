The Natronomonas pharaonis DSM 2160 genome has 1 chromosome and 2 plasmids. The sequences are 2,749,696 bp long and contains approximately 2,822 predicted proteins.  N. pharaonis grows at high salt and pH and has multiple systems for nitrogen assimilation. The sequences were completed in Sep 2005 by Max Planck Institute, and was published in Living with two extremes: conclusions from the genome sequence of Natronomonas pharaonis. .  Falb M, Pfeiffer F, Palm P, Rodewald K, Hickmann V, Tittor J, Oesterhelt D.
