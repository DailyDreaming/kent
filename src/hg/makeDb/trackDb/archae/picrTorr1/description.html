The Picrophilus torridus DSM9790 genome sequence is 1,545,900 bp long and contains approximately 1535 protein-coding genes.  P. torridus is a thermoacidophile that can grow at temperatures around 60C. A membrane that is very impermiable to protons allows P. torridus to live at pH as low as 0, while maintaining a pH of 4.6 internally.

The genome was completed in June 2004 by the University of Goettingen and published in

Genome sequence of Picrophilus torridus and its implications for life around pH 0.  Fütterer, O., Angelov, A., Liesegang, H., Gottschalk, G., Schleper, C., Schepers, B., Dock, C., Antranikian, G., Liebl, W.  Proc. Natl. Acad. Sci. USA 2004; 101(24):9091-9096.

Sample position queries

A genome position can be specified by chromosomal coordinate range, COG ID, or keywords from the GenBank or TIGR description of a gene. The following list shows examples of valid position queries. 

Request:
    Genome Browser Response:

chr
Displays all of chromosome (the entire genome).
chr:1-10000
Displays first ten thousand bases on the chromosome.
NADH
Lists genes with "NADH" in the description.
PTO0002
Display chromosome at position of gene PTO0002.
PTO00
Lists genes with names beginning with "PTO00".
