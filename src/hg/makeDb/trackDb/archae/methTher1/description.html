The Methanothermobacter thermautotrophicus str. Delta H genome is 1.75 Million bp long and contains approximately 1920 predicted genes. M. thermoautotrophicus is anaerobic and thermophilic. It grows at pH 7.2-7.6, and produces methane by reducing carbon dioxide.

The sequence was released Aug 1997 by the Oscient Pharmaceuticals Corporation, and was described in J Bacteriol 179:7135-55 (1997) Smith DR, Doucette-Stamm LA, Deloughery C, Lee H, Dubois J, et al.  "Complete genome sequence of Methanobacterium thermoautotrophicum deltaH: functional analysis and comparative genomics. "
Abstract: The complete 1,751,377-bp sequence of the genome of the thermophilic archaeon Methanobacterium thermoautotrophicum deltaH has been determined by a whole-genome shotgun sequencing approach. A total of 1,855 open reading frames (ORFs) have been identified that appear to encode polypeptides, 844 (46%) of which have been assigned putative functions based on their similarities to database sequences with assigned functions. A total of 514 (28%) of the ORF-encoded polypeptides are related to sequences with unknown functions, and 496 (27%) have little or no homology to sequences in public databases.
