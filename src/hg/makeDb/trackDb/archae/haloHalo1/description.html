The Halobacterium Sp. NRC-1 genome has 1 chromosome and 2 plasmids. The total sequences are 2,571,010 bp long and contain approximately 2,622 predicted proteins.  Halobacterium is a halophilic archaeon found under conditions of extremely high salinity, with a NaCl optimum of 4.3 M. This organism grows aerobically. Genetic systems are available for this species. The sequences were completed in Oct 2000 by the University of Massachusetts-Amherst and the University of Washington, and were published in Genome sequence of Halobacterium species NRC-1.  Ng WV, Kennedy SP, Mahairas GG, Berquist B, Pan M, Shukla HD, Lasky SR, Baliga NS, Thorsson V, Sbrogna J, Swartzell S, Weir D, Hall J, Dahl TA, Welti R, Goo YA, Leithauser B, Keller K, Cruz R, Danson MJ, Hough DW, Maddocks DG, Jablonski PE, Krebs MP, Angevine CM, Dale H, Isenbarger TA, Peck RF, Pohlschroder M, Spudich JL, Jung KW, Alam M, Freitas T, Hou S, Daniels CJ, Dennis PP, Omer AD, Ebhardt H, Lowe TM, Liang P, Riley M, Hood L, DasSarma S.
