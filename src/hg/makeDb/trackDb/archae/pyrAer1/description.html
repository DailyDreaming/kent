The Pyrobaculum aerophilum IM2 genome sequence is 2,222,430 bp long and contains approximately 2,587 protein-coding genes.  P. aerophilum is a hyperthermophilic crenarchaeon which was isolated from a boiling marine hole at Martoni Beach, Italy. It grows optimally at 100 C, either in the presence of low amounts of oxygen or anaerobically. It is remarkable for being able to utilize five different oxidants in respiration: oxygen, nitrate, arsenate, selenate, iron (III), and thiosulfate.

The genome was sequenced and annotated by Sorel Fitz-Gibbon, a graduate student in Jeffrey Miller's lab at UCLA (Fitz-Gibbon ST, Ladner H, Kim UJ, Stetter KO, Simon MI, Miller JH, PNAS USA 99:984-9, 2002).

Sample position queries

A genome position can be specified chromosomal coordinate range, COG ID, or keywords from the GenBank or TIGR description of an gene. The following list shows examples of valid position queries for the human genome. 

Request:
    Genome Browser Response:

chr
Displays all of chromosome (the entire genome).
chr:1-10000
Displays first ten thousand bases on the chromosome.
oxidoreductase
Lists all genes with "oxidoreductase" in the description.
PAE1108
Display chromosome at position of gene PAE1108.
PAE110
Lists genes with names beginning with "PAE110" (i.e. PAE1101, PAE1102, etc.).
