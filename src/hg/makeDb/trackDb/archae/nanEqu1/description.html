The Nanoarchaeum equitans genome sequence is 490,885 bp long and contains approximately 552 DNA-coding sequences.  N. equitans is a hyperthermophilic archaeon found as a parasite to Ignicoccus.  The sequence was completed in Oct 2003 by the Diversa Corporation, and was published in The genome of Nanoarchaeum equitans: insights into early archaeal evolution and derived parasitism.. Waters E, Hohn MJ, Ahel I, Graham DE, Adams MD, Barnstead M, Beeson KY, Bibbs L, Bolanos R, Keller M, Kretz K, Lin X, Mathur E, Ni J, Podar M, Richardson T, Sutton GG, Simon M, Soll D, Stetter KO, Short JM, Noordewier M.  Proc. Natl. Acad. Science USA. 2003;100(22):12974-8.

Sample position queries

A genome position can be specified chromosomal coordinate range, COG ID, or keywords from the GenBank or TIGR description of an gene. The following list shows examples of valid position queries for the human genome. 

Request:
    Genome Browser Response:

chr
Displays all of chromosome (the entire genome).
chr:1-10000
Displays first ten thousand bases on the chromosome.
NADH
Lists genes with "NADH" in the description.
NEQ006
Display chromosome at position of gene NEQ006.
NEQ001
Lists genes with names beginning with "NEQ001".
