The Pyrococcus abyssi GE5 genome is 1.77 Million bp long and contains approximately 1995 predicted genes. P. abyssi is a hyperthermophilic archaeon isolated from samples taken close to a hot spring situated 3500 meters deep in the south-east Pacific. The optimal growth conditions are reported as 103C at 200 atmospheres pressure.

The sequence was released June 2001 by Genoscope, and was described in Mol Microbiol 47:1495-512 (2003) Cohen GN, Barbe V, Flament D, Galperin M, Heilig R, et al.  "An integrated analysis of the genome of the hyperthermophilic archaeon Pyrococcus abyssi. "
Abstract: The hyperthermophilic euryarchaeon Pyrococcus abyssi and the related species Pyrococcus furiosus and Pyrococcus horikoshii, whose genomes have been completely sequenced, are presently used as model organisms in different laboratories to study archaeal DNA replication and gene expression and to develop genetic tools for hyperthermophiles. We have performed an extensive re-annotation of the genome of P. abyssi to obtain an integrated view of its phylogeny, molecular biology and physiology. Many new functions are predicted for both informational and operational proteins. Moreover, several candidate genes have been identified that might encode missing links in key metabolic pathways, some of which have unique biochemical features.
