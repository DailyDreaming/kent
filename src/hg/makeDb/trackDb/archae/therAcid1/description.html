The Thermoplasma acidophilum DSM 1728 genome sequence is 1,564,906 bp long and contains approximately 1482 protein-coding genes.  T. acidophilum is a thermophilic, acidophilic, mycoplasma that grows optimally at 59C and pH 2.0. This facultative aerobe and is one of the most acidophilic organisms known.

The genome was completed in October 2000 by the Max Planck Institute and published in

The genome sequence of the thermoacidophilic scavenger Thermoplasma acidophilum.   Ruepp A, Graml W, Santos-Martinez ML, Koretke KK, Volker C, Mewes HW, Frishman D, Stocker S, Lupas AN, Baumeister W.   Nature. 2000; 407(6803):508-13.

Sample position queries

A genome position can be specified by chromosomal coordinate range, COG ID, or keywords from the GenBank or TIGR description of a gene. The following list shows examples of valid position queries. 

Request:
    Genome Browser Response:

chr
Displays all of chromosome (the entire genome).
chr:1-10000
Displays first ten thousand bases on the chromosome.
NADH
Lists genes with "NADH" in the description.
TA0001
Display chromosome at position of gene TA0001.
TA000
Lists genes with names beginning with "TA000".
