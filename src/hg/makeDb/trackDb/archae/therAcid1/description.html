The sequence was released Sep 2000 by the Max Planck Institute, and was described in Nature 407:508-13 (2000) Ruepp A, Graml W, Santos-Martinez ML, Koretke KK, Volker C, et al.  "The genome sequence of the thermoacidophilic scavenger Thermoplasma acidophilum. "
Abstract: Thermoplasma acidophilum is a thermoacidophilic archaeon that thrives at 59 degrees C and pH 2, which was isolated from self-heating coal refuse piles and solfatara fields. Species of the genus Thermoplasma do not possess a rigid cell wall, but are only delimited by a plasma membrane. Many macromolecular assemblies from Thermoplasma, primarily proteases and chaperones, have been pivotal in elucidating the structure and function of their more complex eukaryotic homologues. Our interest in protein folding and degradation led us to seek a more complete representation of the proteins involved in these pathways by determining the genome sequence of the organism.
