The Haloarcula marismortui has 2 chromosomes and 7 plasmids. The total sequences are 4,274,642 bp long and contain approximately 4,242 predicted proteins.  H. marisnortui is a halophilic archaeon found in the dead sea, where it optimally grows at around 4.5 molar salt.  The sequences were completed in Nov 2004 by the Institute for Systems Biology in Seattle, and was published in Genome sequence of Haloarcula marismortui: a halophilic archaeon from the Dead Sea..  Baliga NS, Bonneau R, Facciotti MT, Pan M, Glusman G, Deutsch EW, Shannon P, Chiu Y, Weng RS, Gan RR, Hung P, Date SV, Marcotte E, Hood L, Ng WV.  Genome Res. 2004;14(11):2221-34.

Sample position queries

A genome position can be specified by chromosomal coordinate range, COG ID, or keywords from the GenBank or TIGR description of an gene. This archae has multiply chromosomes possible.

The available chromosome/plasmid names are chr1, chr2, plasmid1, plasmid2, plasmid3, plasmid4, plasmid5, plasmid6, plasmid7.

The following list shows examples of valid position queries for this genome. 

Request:
    Genome Browser Response:

chr2
Displays all of chromosome 2.
plasmid1
Displays all of plasmid 1.
chr1:1-10000
Displays first ten thousand bases on the chromosome.

Lists genes with names beginning with "rrnAC086".
