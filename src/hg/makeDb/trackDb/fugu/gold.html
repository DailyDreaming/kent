Description

This track shows the V3.0 (Aug. 2002) whole genome shotgun assembly of the Fugu rubripes genome, provided by the US DOE Joint Genome Institute.

This assembly was constructed with the JGI assembler, JAZZ, paired end sequencing reads at a coverage of 5.7X produced at JGI, Myriad Genetics, and Celera Genomics. All reads are plasmid, cosmid, or BAC end-sequences, with the predominant coverage coming from 2 kb insert plasmids. The assembly contains 20,379 scaffolds totaling 319 million base pairs; the largest 679 scaffolds total 160 million base pairs.

The scaffolds in the display are ordered numerically, using the scaffold numbering provided by JGI. The UCSC browser depicts the Fugu genome as a single "unordered chromosome", with 1000 base pair "scaffold" gaps that are shown by the Gap track. The scaffolds decrease in size as the scaffold number increases; scaffold #1 is the largest.
