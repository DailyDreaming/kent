Description

MicroRNA target sites in 3' UTRs as predicted by PicTar, based on the 3' UTRs annoatated by WormBase and based on our own "guessed" 3' UTRs (see below).

Methods

The original PicTar algorithm was published in Krek et al, Nature Genetics, 2005. Fly predictions were published in Grun et al 2006. Displayed are updated predictions as published in Lall et al, Current Biology 2006. Briefly, PicTar is a Hidden Markov Model that assigns probabilities to 3' UTR subsequences to be a binding site for a microRNA, considers all possible ways the 3' UTR could be bound by microRNAs, and then uses a maximum likelihood method to compute the optimal Likelihood under which the 3' UTR could be explained by microRNAs and background. The Score is this likelihood divided by backround, ie the local basecomposition of each 3' UTR is taken into account. To fit the Score track conventions of the UCSC browser (integers), we scaled all scores by the maximum score of all microRNA:3' UTR scores observed. Note that PicTar scores any 3' UTR that has at least 1 aligned, conserved predicted binding site for a microRNA, but then incorporates all possible binding sites into the score, even if they appear to be non-conserved. Since the Score for a 3' UTR is a "phylo" average over all orthologous 3' UTRs used, "scattered" sites that appear in many species may boost the score, and individual sites shown in the display may not be aligned and conserved in all species under consideration.

For the nematode target predictions, three nematode species were used (elegans, briggsae, remanei). Since 3' UTRs were only relatively rarely annotated in WormBase or RefSeq, we guessed for all transcripts without annotated 3' UTRs the 3' UTR sequences by extending the CDS by 500 bases downstream of the STOP codon, but stopped when hitting another CDS.

For a detailed analysis regarding costruction of 3' UTRs and signal to noise ratios and and sensitivity please refer to Lall et al Current biology 2006.
