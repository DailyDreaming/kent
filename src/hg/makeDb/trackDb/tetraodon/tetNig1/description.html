The Tetraodon nigroviridis V7 (February 2004) assembly was provided Genoscope, Evry, France and it results from a collaboration between Genoscope and the Eli and Edythe L. The project was supported by the Consortium National de Recherche en Genomique and the National Human Genome Research Institute (NHGRI).

Sample position queries

A genome position can be specified by the accession number of an mRNA or EST, a chromosome range, or keywords from the GenBank description of an mRNA. The following list provides examples of various types of position queries for the Tetraodon genome. Note that some position queries (e.g. "huntington") may return matches to the mRNA records of other species. In these cases, the mRNAs are mapped to their homologs in Tetraodon. See the User Guide for more help.

Assembly details

This assembly was constructed with using Whole Genome Shotgun (WGS) resulting in a sequence coverage of about 7.9X. This assembly contains 45,609 contigs and 25,773 scaffolds generated by the Arachne program, and covers >90% of the genome. Additional linking data were used to build ultracontigs and to organize the assembly into chromosomes. The size of the Tetraodon genome is estimated, by Genoscope, to be about 385 Mb.

Downloads of the Tetraodon data and annotations may be obtained from the UCSC FTP site or Downloads page.
