Description

MicroRNA target sites in 3' UTRs as predicted by PicTar, based on the full length cDNAs provided by flybase.

Methods

The original PicTar algorithm was published in Krek et al, Nature Genetics, 2005. Displayed are updated predictions as published in Lall et al, Current Biology 2006. Briefly, PicTar is a Hidden Markov Model that assigns probabilities to 3' UTR subsequences to be a binding site for a microRNA, considers all possible ways the 3' UTR could be bound by microRNAs, and then uses a maximum likelihood method to compute the optimal Likelihood under which the 3' UTR could be explained by microRNAs and background. The Score is this likelihood divided by backround, ie the local basecomposition of each 3' UTR is taken into account. To fit the Score track conventions of the UCSC browser (integers), we scaled all scores by the maximum score of all microRNA:3' UTR scores observed. Note that PicTar scores any 3' UTR that has at least 1 aligned, conserved predicted binding site for a microRNA, but then incorporates all possible binding sites into the score, even if they appear to be non-conserved. Since the Score for a 3' UTR is a "phylo" average over all orthologous 3' UTRs used, "scattered" sites that appear in many species may boost the score, and individual sites shown in the display may not be aligned and conserved in all species under consideration.

Two levels of conservation can be chosen: conservation between melanogaster/yakuba/ananassae/pseudoobscura (high sensitivity settings) and conservation between the melanogaster/yakuba/ananassae/pseudoobscura/mojavensis/virilis. The latter settings have improved specificity, but less sensitivity.

For a detailed analysis of signal to noise ratios and and sensitivity please refer to Grun et al, Plos Computational Biology, and Lall et al 2006.
