Description

This track shows the Zebrafish Zv3 (Nov. 2003) assembly provided by the The Wellcome Trust Sanger Institute. The assembly was constructed with the assembler, Phusion, from paired end sequencing reads resulting in a sequence coverage of about 5.7X. This assembly contains 58,339 scaffolds (supercontigs) totaling 1.5 billion base pairs.

For the clone based mapping and finishing, clones from different libraries were fingerprinted by digestion with the HindIII restriction enzyme. From the information produced in this way, overlapping clones were linked into FingerPrinted Contigs (FPCs). Next, clones from a tiling path through the FPC contigs are being selected for high quality sequencing and the resulting sequence is submitted to EMBL/Genbank. The supercontigs tied to the FPC map create the assembly shown in this track. The finished clone sequence is then analyzed via a pipeline that includes repeatmasking, ab initio gene prediction and blast searches against all protein, EST and cDNA sequences that are available. Results from this analysis are used to manually annotate clones with gene structures, descriptions and poly-A features. At this point, the clone is submitted to EMBL/Genbank again and can be browsed in Vega.

In dense mode, this track depicts the path through the draft and finished clones (aka the golden path) used to create the assembled sequence. Clone boundaries are distinguished by the use of alternating gold and brown coloration. Where gaps exist in the path, spaces are shown between the gold and brown blocks. If the relative order and orientation of the contigs between the two blocks is known, a line is drawn to bridge the blocks.

All components within this track are of fragment type "W": Whole Genome Shotgun contig except for those in chrFinished which are of type "F": Finished.

The Genome Browser depicts the zebrafish genome as 25 chromsomes consisting of WGS supercontigs that were mapped to an FPC contig and were from a known chromosome. There are also 3 unordered virtual chromosomes namely chrUn, chrFinished and chrNA. These 3 chromosomes have 1000 bp scaffold gaps, which are shown in the Gap track annotation.

Unordered Virtual Chromosomes:

  • chrNA - WGS contigs that could not be related to any FPC contig
  • chrFinished - Finished clones that could not be place into WGS supercontigs
  • chrUn - WGS supercontigs that mapped to FPC contigs and the chromosome is unknown
