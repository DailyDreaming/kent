Description

This track is produced as part of the ENCODE Project. This track displays transcriptional fragments associated with RNA binding proteins in different cell lines, using RIP-Seq(Ribonomic) profiling via Illumina SBS. The RBP Assoc RNA view shows the genomic location of sequencing peaks. Data for this track was produced as part of the Encyclopedia of DNA Elements (ENCODE) Project,

In eukaryotic organisms gene regulatory networks require an additional level of coordination that links transcriptional and post-transcriptional processes. Messenger RNAs have traditionally been viewed as passive molecules in the pathway from transcription to translation. However, it is now clear that RNA-binding proteins play a major role in regulating multiple mRNAs in order to facilitate gene expression patterns. These tracks show the associated mRNAs that co-precipitate with the targeted RNA-binding proteins using RIP-Seq profiling.

Methods

RBP-mRNA complexes were purified from cells grown according to the approved ENCODE cell culture protocols . RNA samples were amplified and converted to cDNA with the Nugen Ovation© RNA-Seq System and prepped for sequencing with the Illumina mRNA-Seq protocol (beginning at the end repair step). Sequencing was done using 36 cycle Illumina single end reads. Resulting libraries were ~23 million and ~35 million reads for K562 and GM12878 respectively.
Libraries were analyzed using tophat version 1.0.14 with bowtie version 0.12.5
Peak tracks were developed as follows:
A) .wig files from tophat were normalized by "average mapped reads per base".
B) Peaks were called from these normalized files using thresholds of
   i) at or above 60th percentile signal
   ii) max gap = 24(read length-1/3)
   iii) min run = 48(read length + 1/3)
C) Unions of overlapping peak regions from total RNA replicates are presented with pVal from a one tailed t-test for average signal from replicates versus 0, no cut-off was used for totals.
D) Intersections of overlapping (across replicates) positive RIP treatment peaks are presented with pVal from one tailed t-test versus signal for same region in negative control replicates. RIP peaks must meet two criteria:
   i) They must be equal or longer than the minimum fragment expected based on the Illumina mRNA-Seq protocol size selection (in this case, expected to be 120b)
   ii) Their pVal must be less than .05
E) For both totals and RIPs, the peak scores are calculated as:
   i)80% weighting given to pVal, scaled as 0.05(or higher) to 0.0 = 0 to 800
   ii)20% weighting given to (average treatment signal minus average control signal)/(maximum treatment vs control difference amongst all peaks)
Note: while not used for cut-off thresholding or score, qVals based on Benjamini Hochberg are also reported.
      Also, an f-test was performed prior to all t-tests and used to determine if t-test should be applied for equal or unequal variance.

For additional RIP methods detail, see Tenenbaum et al. 2002; Baroni et al. 2008; Penalva et al. 2004, below.

Verification

All experiments (including controls) performed in and analyzed as duplicates.
