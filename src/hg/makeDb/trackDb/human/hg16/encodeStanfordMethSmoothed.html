Description

This track displays smoothed (sliding-window mean) scores for experimentally determined regions of unmethylated CpGs in the ENCODE regions. These experiments were performed in 8 cell lines, each of which is displayed as a separate subtrack:

Methods

High molecular weight genomic DNA was prepared from each cell line. The genomic DNA was digested with a cocktail of 6 methyl-sensitive restriction enzymes (AciI, HhaI, BstUI, HpaII, HgaI, and HpyCH4IV) and size selected to deplete the genome of unmethylated regions. Digested and undigested DNA (control) were amplified, labeled, and hybridized to oligo tiling arrays produced by Nimblegen. The data for each array were median subtracted (log 2 ratios) and normalized (divided by the standard deviation).

The transformed mean ratios of undigested:digested genomic DNA for all probes were then smoothed by calculating a sliding-window mean. Windows of 6 neighboring probes (sliding 2 probes at a time) were used; within each window, the highest and lowest value were dropped, and the remaining 4 values were averaged. In order to increase the contrast between high and low values for visual display, the average was converted to a score by this formula:
score = 8^(average) * 10
These scores are for visualization purposes; for all analyses, the raw ratios should be used. Those ratios are available in the "Stanf Meth" track.

Verification

Three biological replicates and 2 technical replicates were done for each of the 8 cell lines. The Myers lab is currently testing the specificity and sensitivity using real-time PCR.
