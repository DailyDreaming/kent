Description

Human genome p53 transcription factor binding sites (TFBS) were surveyed by adopting a ChIP-PET based sequencing approach that combines chromatin immunoprecipitation (ChIP) with paired-end di-tag (PET) sequencing. This track shows PET sequences derived from 65,572 individual p53 ChIP fragments of 5FU-stimulated HCT116 cells.

In the display, the actual PET sequences are represented by blocks. Each ChIP fragment is defined by two boundary blocks connected by a thin arrowed line. The PET singletons from non-specific ChIP fragments are discretely located along the genome as background. Overlapping PET clusters (PET fragments that overlap one another) originating from the ChIP enrichment process define the genomic loci that are potential p53 binding sites.

Methods

The ChIP-enriched DNA fragments were cloned into a plasmid vector, and then converted into paired-end ditags (PET) for concatenation, cloning, and sequencing. PET sequences containing 18 bp from the 5' end and 18 bp from the 3' end of the original ChIP fragments were extracted from raw sequence reads and mapped to the genome independently for demarcating the ChIP DNA fragments. The following specific mapping criteria were used:

  • both 5' and 3' signatures must be present on the same chromosome
  • orientation must be correct
  • a minimal 17 bp match must exist
  • the genomic distance between ends must be within 4 Kb

Verification

40 genomic locations identified by PET-3+ clusters were randomly selected and subjected to quantitative real-time PCR analyses. The relative enrichment of candidate regions compared to control GST ChIP DNA was determined and all 40 regions (100%) were confirmed to have significant enrichment of p53 ChIP, indicating that all these regions are true p53-bound targets.
