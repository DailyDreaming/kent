Description

Genomic Evolutionary Rate Profiling (GERP) constrained element annotations describe regions of the human genome (throughout the ENCODE target regions) that exhibit significant evidence of the effects of purifying selection. Elements are scored according to the inferred intensity of purifying selection, and are measured as "rejected substitutions" (RS). RS capture the magnitude of difference between the number of "observed" substitutions (estimated using maximum likelihood) and the number that would be "expected" under a neutral model of evolution.

Analyses are based on alignments of human ENCODE genomic DNA to many other mammals using the LAGAN alignment package.

Methods

Alignments, generated target by target for all ENCODE regions, are first compressed so that the human sequence is ungapped. Maximum likelihood rate estimation is used to estimate an "observed" rate of evolution for each column, given a fixed species topology relating the sequences, where gapped species are eliminated from consideration. A neutral tree that describes the average rates of evolution (in terms of nucleotide substitutions affecting neutral sites) over all the branches of the species topology relating the analyzed sequence is used to generate an estimate of the "expected" rate of evolution for each column, where gapped species are eliminated.

"Constrained elements" are identified as those groups of consecutive human bases that have an observed rate of evolution that is smaller than the expected rate. These groups of columns are merged if they are less than a few nucleotides apart, and scored according to the sum of the site-by-site difference between observed and expected rates (RS).

Verification

We analyze permutations of the actual alignments, and treat "constrained elements" identified in these permuted alignments as "false positives". We subsequently pick an RS threshold such that the total length of "false positive" (that is, identified in the permuted alignments) constrained elements is less than 5% of the length of constrained elements identified in the actual alignment. Thus, all annotated constrained elements are significant at better than 95% confidence, and the total fraction of the ENCODE regions annotated as constrained is 5-7%.
