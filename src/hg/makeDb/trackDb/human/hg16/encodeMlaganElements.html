Description

Genomic Evolutionary Rate Profiling (GERP) constrained elements exhibit significant evidence of the effects of purifying selection. Elements are scored according to the inferred intensity of purifying selection and are measured as "rejected substitutions" (RSs). RSs capture the magnitude of difference between the number of "observed" substitutions (estimated using maximum likelihood) and the number that would be "expected" under a neutral model of evolution. The RS is displayed as part of the item name, and items with higher RSs are displayed in a darker shade of blue. The score shown on the details page is generally not as accurate as the RS count that is part of the item name.

Analyses are based on alignments of human ENCODE genomic DNA to many other mammals using the LAGAN alignment package.

Methods

The GERP method relies on maximal likelihood to estimate the difference between observed and expected substitution rates. The expected and observed rates are both calculated on a phylogenic tree using the same fixed topology. The branch lengths of the expected tree are based on the average substitutions at neutral sites. The branch lengths of the observed tree, which is calculated separately for each human base, are based on the substitutions seen at the column of the multiple alignment at that base. Species that have gaps at a particular column are not considered in the estimation for that column.

"Constrained elements" are identified as those groups of consecutive human bases that have an observed rate of evolution that is smaller than the expected rate. These groups of columns are merged if they are less than a few nucleotides apart and are scored according to the sum of the site-by-site difference between observed and expected rates (RS).

Verification

Permutations of the actual alignments were analyzed, and "constrained elements" identified in these permuted alignments were treated as "false positives". Subsequently, an RS threshold was picked such that the total length of "false positive" constrained elements (identified in the permuted alignments) was less than 5% of the length of constrained elements identified in the actual alignment. Thus, all annotated constrained elements are significant at better than 95% confidence, and the total fraction of the ENCODE regions annotated as constrained is 5-7%.
