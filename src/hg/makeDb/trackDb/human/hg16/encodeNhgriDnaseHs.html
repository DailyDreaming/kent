Description

This track displays DNaseI-hypersensitive sites in CD4+ T-cells before and after activation. DNaseI-hypersensitive sites are associated with gene regulatory regions, particularly for upregulated genes. CD4+ T-cells, also known as helper or inducer T cells, are involved in generating an immune response. CD4+ T-cells are also one of the primary targets of the HIV virus.

The top subtrack corresponds to unactivated T cells; the bottom subtrack shows activated T cells. Within the subtracks, the gray and black lines represent probable hypersensitive sites. The darker the lines, the more likely the site is to be hypersensitive.

Methods

DNaseI-hypersensitive sites were cloned from primary human CD4+ T-cells and sequenced using massively parallel signature sequencing (Brenner et al., 2000; Crawford and Collins, unpublished data). Only those clusters of multiple DNaseI library sequences that map within 500 bases of each other are displayed. Each cluster has a unique identifier, visible when the track is displayed in full or packed mode. The last digit of each identifier represents the number of sequences that map within that particular cluster.

Verification

Real-time PCR assay was used to verify valid DNaseI-hypersensitive sites. Approximately 50% of clusters of two sequences are valid. These clusters are shown in light gray. 80% of clusters of three sequences are valid, indicated by dark gray. 100% of clusters of four or more sequences are valid, shown in black.

This data set includes confirmed elements for 35 of the 44 ENCODE regions. It is estimated that these data identify 10-20% of all hypersensitive sites within CD4+ T cells. Further sequencing will be required to identify additional sites.
