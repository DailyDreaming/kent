Description

Genomic Evolutionary Rate Profiling (GERP) scores describe nucleotide substitution rates observed in alignments containing all human bases in the ENCODE portion of the genome. Scores are estimated on a column-by-column basis using multiple sequence alignments of mammalian genomic DNA generated by the LAGAN alignment package.

The scores range from 0 to 3, with higher scores corresponding to human bases in alignment columns with higher degrees of similarity, i.e. bases that have evolved slowly, some of which have been under purifying selection. The opposite holds true for swiftly evolving (low similarity) columns.

Methods

The GERP score is the expected substitution rate divided by the observed substitution rate at a particular human base. Scores greater than 3 are clipped to 3. The expected and observed rates are both calculated on a phylogenic tree using the same fixed topology. The branch lengths of the expected tree are based on the average substitutions at neutral sites. The branch lengths of the observed tree, which is calculated separately for each human base, are based on the substitutions seen at the column of the multiple alignment at that base. Species that have gaps at a particular column are not considered in the scoring for that column.

Verification

Scores are deterministic, given a maximum-likelihood model of nucleotide substitution, species topology, neutral tree, and alignment.
