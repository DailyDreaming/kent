Description

Genomic Evolutionary Rate Profiling (GERP) scores describe nucleotide substitution rates observed in alignments containing all human bases in the ENCODE portion of the genome. Scores are estimated on a column-by-column basis using multiple sequence alignments of mammalian genomic DNA generated by the LAGAN alignment package.

The range of scores is from 0 to 3, with higher scores corresponding to human bases in alignment columns with higher degrees of similarity (bases that have evolved slowly, some of which have been under purifying selection), while the opposite holds true for swiftly evolving (low similarity) columns.

Methods

Alignments, generated target by target for all ENCODE regions, are first compressed so that the human sequence is ungapped. Maximum likelihood rate estimation is used to estimate an "observed" rate of evolution for each column, given a fixed species topology relating the sequences, where gapped species are eliminated from consideration. A neutral tree that describes the average rates of evolution (in terms of nucleotide substitutions affecting neutral sites) over all the branches of the species topology relating the analyzed sequence is used to generate an estimate of the "expected" rate of evolution for each column, where gapped species are eliminated. The scores plotted reflect the inverse of the ratio of "observed" to "expected", so higher scores (capped so that columns of identical residues are scored at 3) show high levels of similarity.

Verification

Scores are deterministic given a maximum-likelihood model of nucleotide substitution, species topology, neutral tree, and alignment.
