Description

This track shows the locations of Transcriptionally Active Regions (TARs) / Transcribed Fragments (transfrags) for Human Neutrophil (PMN) Total RNA hybridized to the Affymetrix ENCODE oligonucleotide microarray. This array has 25 mer oligonucleotide probes tiled approximately every 21 bp covering all the non-repetitive DNA sequence of the ENCODE regions. The transcript map is a combined signal for both strands of DNA. This is derived from 10 different biological samples (each sample was extracted from a different individual), each with at least 2 technical replicates. See NCBI GEO GSE2678 for details of experimental protocols.

Methods

The data from technical replicates were median scaled and quantile normalized to each other. Using a 101 bp sliding window centered on each oligonucleotide probe a signal map (estimating RNA abundance) is generated by computing the pseudomedian signal of all PM-MM pairs (median of pairwise PM-MM averages) within the window (including replicates). Biological replicate signal maps are combined by quantile normalizing them between replicates and computing the median signal at each oligonucleotide probe location. Ten independant biological replicates were generated and each was hybridized to at least two different arrays (technical replicates). Transcribed regions (TARs/transfrags) are then identified using a signal theshold of 90 percentile of signal intensities as well as a maximum gap of 50 bp and a minimum run of 50 bp (between oligonucleotide positions).

Verification

Transcribed regions (TARs/transfrags) as determined by individual biological samples are compared to ensure significant overlap.
