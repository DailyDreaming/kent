Description

This track displays expression levels of first and a constitutive exons of genes in ENCODE regions, based on a real competitive Polymerase Chain Reaction (rcPCR) technique described in Ding et al. 2003. Expression levels are indicated by color, ranging from black (no expression) to red (high expression). Experiments were performed on total RNA samples of ten normal human tissues, purchased from Clontech (Palo Alto, CA). The tissues are cerebral cortex, colon, heart, kidney, liver, lung, skeletal muscle, spleen, stomach, and testis.

Methods

The rcPCR technique combines competitive PCR and Matrix-assisted laser desorption/ionization time-of-flight mass spectrometry (MALDI-TOF MS) for gene expression analysis. To measure the expression level of a gene, an oligonucleotide standard (60-80 bases; its concentration is known) complementary to the target sequence, except for a single base mismatch in the middle, is added as the competitor for PCR. The gene of interest and the oligonucleotide standard resemble two alleles of a heterozygous locus in an allele frequency analysis experiment, and thus can be analyzed with the quantified by the high-throughput MALDI-TOF MS based MassARRAY system (Sequenom Inc.). After PCR, a base extension reaction is carried out with an extension primer, a ThermoSequenase and a mixture of ddNTPs/dNTP. The extension primer anneals to the immediate 5 ’-upstream sequence of the mismatch position. Depending on the nature of the mismatch and the mixture composition of ddNTPs/dNTP, one or two bases are added to the extension primer, producing two extension products with one base-length difference. These two extension products are then detected and quantified by the high-throughput MALDI-TOF MS based MassARRAY system (Sequenom Inc.).

Alternative transcription start sites (TSS) for 20 ENCODE genes were predicted using our in-house computational tool PromoSer (http://biowulf.bu.edu/zlab/Promoser/). PromoSer computationally identifies the TSS by considering alignments of a large number of partial and full-length mRNA sequences and ESTs to genomic DNA, with provision for alternative promoters. In PromoSer, the treatment of alternative first exons (or the resulting TSSs) is as follows. All transcripts (mRNA, full length mRNA and EST) deemed to belong to the same gene cluster are examined. Individual ESTs were not considered for alternative TSSs and only the 5' most position from all ESTs in the cluster was considered a potential TSS. If multiple 5'-end positions were more than 20 bp apart, they were reported as alternative TSSs.

For each gene, we identified all alternative first exons based on manual selections of PromoSer predictions. We also selected an exon that is shared by all transcripts (called the constitutive exon). It should be noted that the amplicons are designed according to transcript sequences and can span a large distance on the genomic sequence. In addition, some amplicons are designed across the junctions between first exons and the constitutive second exons, and thus these amplicons may overlap with the amplicons that correspond to the constitutive second exons.

The nomenclature of each alternative transcript is such that the gene name is followed by an identifier for the alternative first exon or the constitutive exon. For example, for gene CAV1, there are three alternative first exons (CAV1-E1A, CAV1-E1B, and CAV1-E1C) and the third exon is chosen as the constitutively expressed exon (CAV1-E3). The absolute expression levels of all exons are individually quantified by rcPCR by designing four assays with PCR amplicons corresponding to each exon. Their expression ratios (CAV1-E1A/CAV1-E3, CAV1-E1B/CAV1-E3, CAV1-E1C/CAV1-E3) indicate the relative abundance of the three alternative first exons. 10 different tissue types are used for the expression analysis. We used 18S rRNA for exon absolute expression normalization among different tissues.

Values shown on this track represent the relative abundance of the alternative first exons with respect to the 18S rRNA. The values have been scaled to show graded colors on the browser.

Verification

One biological replicate has been performed for all the genes. Two to four competitor concentrations have been used to detect the expression level of each exon. Two to six technical replicates have been performed for each competitor concentration. We plan to do one more biological replicate in the future.
