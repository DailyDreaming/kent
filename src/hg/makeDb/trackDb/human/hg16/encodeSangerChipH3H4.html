DESCRIPTION

Genome-wide location analysis of histone H3 monomethyl lysine 4 (H3K4me1) was conducted employing ChIP/chip using chromatin extracted from GM06990 (lymphoblastoid) cells. Histone methylation serves as a stable genomic imprint that regulates gene expression and other epigenetic phenomena. Methylation of lysine four on histone H3 is found in transcriptionally active domains called euchromatin.

Cell line: GM06990
Antibody: ab8580
Epitope: H3K4me1
DNA microarray: ENCODE3.1.1

METHODS

Chromatin from the cell line was cross-linked with 1% formaldehyde, precipitated with antibody binding to histone H3 monomethyl lysine 4, sheared and hybridized to a DNA array. DNA was not amplified prior to hybridization. The raw and transformed data files reflect fold enrichment over background, averaged over six replicates.

VERIFICATION

There are six replicates total: two technical replicates (immunoprecipitations) for each of the three biological replicates (cell cultures). Raw and transformed (averaged) data can be downloaded from the Wellcome Trust Sanger Institute ftp site.
