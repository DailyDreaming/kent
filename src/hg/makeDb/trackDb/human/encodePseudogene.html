Description

This track shows pseudogenes in the ENCODE regions derived from taking a consensus of 5 different methods (Yale Pipeline, GenCode manual annotation, two different UCSC methods, and GIS). This is the 6-Jan-2006 Freeze and contains 201 consensus pseudogenes. Pseudogenes are defined in this analysis as genomic sequences that are similar to known proteins with various inactivating disablements ( e.g. premature stop codons or frameshifts) in their "putative" protein coding regions. Pseudogenes are flagged as either recently processed or non-processed.

Methods

We started with lists of pseudogenes from four research groups: GIS, HAVANA/GENCODE, UCSC, and Yale . Roughly, we followed the outlined below:

  • Step I: The above lists were filtered to remove pseudogenes that overlap with current GENCODE coding exons/loci. Pseudogenes overlapping with introns or noncoding genes were kept. Subsequent filtering of pseudogenes removed those overlapping with exons of Known Genes (except for the HAVANA list).
  • Step II: A union of the pseudogenes from each filtered list was created. If a pseudogenic region was annotated by more than one group, the boundary represented the smallest start and the largest end.
  • Step III: A parent protein for each pseudogene in the union was assigned using a protein set from UniProt. Pseudogenes without a matching protein were excluded.
  • Step IV: Each pseudogene was then re-aligned to its parent protein.
  • Step V: The consensus list of pseudogenes was updated with boundaries derived from the alignment in Step IV.
  • Step VI: The consensus list of pseudogenes was updated with their assigned parent proteins and new classification (processed or non-processed).

Verification

All pseudogenes in the list have been extensively curated by Adam Frankish and Jenifer Harrow at the The Wellcome Trust Sanger Institute.
