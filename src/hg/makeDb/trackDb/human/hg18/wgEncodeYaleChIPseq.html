Description

These tracks show probable binding sites of the specified transcription factors in the given cell types as determined by chromatin immunoprecipitation followed by high throughput sequencing (CHIP-seq). The tracks are grouped by TF targeted antibody and by cell type. Included for each cell type is the input signal, which represents the control condition where no antibody targeting was performed. Within a grouping their will be one or more of the following:

  • Raw Signal of aligned read density
  • Signal of replicate combined and input (control) subtracted read density
  • Called Peaks which represent the sites with the greatest evidence of TF binding obtained from these experiments.

Methods

Similar to those previously published with the exceptions that the cells were unstimulated and sodium orthovanadate was omitted from the buffers: Euskirchen et al., Nature 2007.

Extraction protocol

HeLa S3 cells were grown either: (PolII and Input) at the National Cell Culture Center (Minneapolis, MN ) in Joklik?s modified MEM supplemented 5% Newborn Calf Serum to a density of 5 x10^5 cells/ml.; or (Stat1 and Input) in Minimum Essential Medium modified for Suspension (SMEM, Invitrogen), supplemented with glutamine, 10% FBS, and antibiotics (penicillin-streptomycin) at 37C in 5% CO2, to a density of 5 x10^5 cells/ml and then stimulated at these same growth conditions for 30 min with 5 ng/ml human recombinant IFN-? (R&D Systems #285-IF).

All cells were then were fixed with 1% formaldehyde at room temperature for 10 min. Fixation was terminated with 125 mM glycine, and cells were washed twice in cold 1x PBS, and then swelled on ice in hypotonic lysis buffer (20 mM Hepes, pH 7.9, 10 mM KCl, 1 mM EDTA, pH 8, 10% glycerol, 1 mM DTT, 0.5 mM PMSF, 0.1 mM sodium orthovanadate and Roche complete protease inhibitors). Cell lysates were homogenized with 30 strokes in a Dounce homogenizer (Kontes, pestle B). Nuclear pellets were collected and lysed in 1x RIPA buffer (10 mM Tris-Cl, pH 8.0, 140 mM NaCl, 1% Triton X-100, 0.1% SDS, 1% deoxycholic acid, 0.5 mM PMSF, 1 mM DTT, 0.1 mM sodium orthovanadate and protease inhibitors). Chromatin was sheared with a Branson 250 Sonifier (output setting 2, 100% duty cycle, seven 30-second pulses) to an average of 700 bp as verified on a 1.5% agarose gel.

Clarified lysates for ChIP samples were agitated overnight at 4C with antibody. Following this incubation Protein A agarose beads (Upstate-Millipore #16-156) were added and allowed to bind for 60 min at 4C. Immunoprecipitates were washed three times in 1x RIPA, once in 1x PBS, and then eluted in 1xTE/1%SDS. Clarified lysates for Input DNA samples were stored overnight at 4C in parallel with ChIP experiments.

For ChIP and Input samples, crosslinks were reversed overnight at 65C. ChIP DNA was purified by incubation with RNase A for 1 h at 37C, with 200 ug/ml Proteinase K for 2 h at 45C, phenol:chloroform:isoamyl alcohol extraction, and precipitation with 0.1 volumes of 3M sodium acetate, 2 volumes of 100% ethanol and 1.5 ul of pellet paint (Novagen).

ChIP-DNA sequencing libraries were prepared according to Illumina's instructions accompanying the DNA Sample Kit (Part# 0801-0303).

Scoring of ChIP-Seq Data

Only sequence reads (length ~30 bps) that align uniquely (using Eland) with at most two mismatches against the hg18 build of the human genome are used. From the mapped tags a signal map of ChIP DNA fragments (average fragment length ~ 200 bps) is constructed where the signal height is the number of overlapping fragments at each nucleotide position in the genome. For each 1Mb segment of each chromosome a peak height threshold is determined by requiring a false discovery rate <= 0.05 when comparing the number of peaks above threshold as compared the number obtained from multiple simulations of a random null background with the same number of mapped reads (also accounting for the fraction of mapable bases for sequence tags in that 1Mb segment). The number of mapped tags in a putative binding region is compared against the normalized (normalized by correlating tag counts in genomic 10 Kb windows) number of mapped tags in the same region from an Input DNA control. Using a binomial test only regions that have a p-value <= 0.05 are considered to be significantly enriched compared to the Input DNA control.
