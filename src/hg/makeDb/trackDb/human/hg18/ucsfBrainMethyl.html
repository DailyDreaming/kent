Description

Genome wide methylation (MeDIP-seq and MRE-seq), histone H4 lysine 4 trimethylation (H3K4me3) and gene expression (RNA-seq) data were generated from primary human brain tissue to investigate the role that intragenic tissue-specific CpG island methylation plays in controlling gene expression (Maunakea et al. 2010)

Methods

DNA, RNA and native chromatin were extracted using standard methods. Methylated DNA immunoprecipitation and sequencing (MeDIP-seq) uses antibody-based immunoprecipitation of 5-methylcytosine (1 mg/ml, Eurogentec, catalog # BI-MECY-0100) and sequencing to map the methylated fraction of the genome. Methyl-sensitive restriction enzyme-seq (MRE-seq) identifies unmethylated CpG sites at single CpG site resolution by sequencing size-selected fragments from parallel DNA digestions with the MREs HpaII, Hin6I, and AciI (Fermentas). Chromatin immunoprecipitation was carried out either with anti-histone H3 trimethyl K4 (H3K4me3) antibody (ab8580, Abcam), or normal rabbit IgG antibody (12-370, Upstate Biotechnology) to assess fold enrichment. A ChIP-seq library was constructed as previously described1 using 11-35 ng of immunoprecipitated DNA. For RNA-seq, 100 ng of total RNA was used to synthesize full-length single-stranded cDNAs using the SMART PCR cDNA Synthesis Kit (Clontech) following the protocol as described by Morin et al2. MRE-seq, MeDIP-seq, H3K4me3 ChIP-seq and RNA-seq libraries were sequenced with an Illumina Genome Analyzer II. Computational and statistical analyses were performed with R package and ad hoc programs.

Verification

MeDIP enrichment was verified by 4 locus-specific PCRs (2 methylated and 2 unmethylated loci). MeDIP-seq and MRE-seq methylation profiles met expectations for methylation patterns at loci with known methylation states, and reads were infrequent in ~1 kb regions devoid of the dinucleotide CpG.
