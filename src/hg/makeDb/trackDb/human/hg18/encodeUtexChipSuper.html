Overview

This super track groups related tracks of ChIP data generated by the UT Austin (The Iyer Laboratory).

Two technologies are presented in this super track: ChIP-chip and ChIP-STAGE. ChIP-chip, also known as genome-wide location analysis, is a technique for isolation and identification of DNA sequences bound by specific proteins in cells. Insteading of detecting bound fragments by microarray, ChIP-STAGE uses Sequence Tag Analysis of Genomic Enrichment, or STAGE, technology by cloning STAGE tags, sequenceing and mapping to the human genome.

These tracks contain ChIP data for several transcription factors, including c-Myc, E2F4 and STAT1, in 2091 foreskin fibroblasts and HeLa cells.
