Overview

This super track groups related tracks of ChIP-chip and ChIP-seq data generated by the Stanford group.

ChIP-chip, also known as genome-wide location analysis, is a technique for isolation and identification of DNA sequences bound by specific proteins in cells. ChIP-seq is a new approach for investigating entire transcription factor-DNA interactomes. It is comprised of chromatin immunoprecipitation (ChIP) followed by single-molecule-based sequencing (seq). ChIP-seq avoids the complications of array hybridization.

Stanford ChIP-chip tracks contain data for Sp1 and Sp3 in multiple cell lines, such as HCT 116.

Stanford ChIP-seq track contains data for NRSF/REST in Jurkat T cell line.
