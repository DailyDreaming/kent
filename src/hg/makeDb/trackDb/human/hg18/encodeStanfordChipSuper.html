Overview

This super track groups related tracks from Stanford ChIP-chip and ChIP-seq experiments for multiple transcription factors in multiple cell lines. Data are displayed as bound region and smoothed scores.

Stanford NRSF

This track shows 25mer sequence reads obtained via a ChIP-seq protocol from an NRSF/REST-enriched ChIP sample and a companion control sample of the same fixed chromatin without immuno-enrichment. These sequence reads were later analyzed using a peak locator (data to be available in the future) to identify NRSF-positive binding events.

ChIP-seq is a new approach for investigating entire transcription factor-DNA interactomes. It is comprised of chromatin immunoprecipitation (ChIP) followed by single-molecule-based sequencing (seq). ChIP-seq avoids the complications of array hybridization.

Chromatin immunoprecipitation was applied to NRSF/REST loci identified by a PSFM (position specific frequency matrix) computational screen. NRSF/REST is a zinc finger repressor that negatively regulates many neuronal genes in stem and progenitor cells and in nonneuronal cell types such as the Jurkat T cell line used for this track.

NRSF (neuron-restrictive silencer factor), also known as REST (repressor element-1 silencing transcription factor), was chosen because prior studies provide a large set of target genes. In addition, the DNA motif bound by NRSF (known as NRSE/RE1) is long (21bp) and well-specified, and there is a high-quality monoclonal antibody that recognizes NRSF efficiently in ChIP experiments.
