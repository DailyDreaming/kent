Description

The CCDS track shows the $organism genome high-confidence gene annotations from the Consensus CDS (CCDS) project. This project is a collaborative effort to identify a core set of human protein coding regions that are consistently annotated and of high quality. The long-term goal is to support convergence towards a standard set of gene annotations on the human genome.

Collaborators include:

  • European Bioinformatics Institute (EBI)
  • National Center for Biotechnology Information (NCBI)
  • University of California, Santa Cruz (UCSC)
  • Wellcome Trust Sanger Institute (WTSI)

Methods

  1. CDS annotations of the $organism genome are obtained from two sources:
    • NCBI RefSeq
    • A union of the gene annotations from Ensembl and Vega, collectively known as Hinxton.
  2. Genes with identical CDS genomic coordinates in both sets become CCDS candidates.
  3. Genes undergo a quality evaluation, which must be approved by all collaborators. Genes are currently assessed using the following criteria:
    • have an initiating ATG, a valid stop codon, and no in-frame stop codons.
    • can be translated from the genome without frameshifts
    • have consensus splice sites
    • don't intersect putative pseudogene predictions
    • have supporting transcripts and protein homology
    • have conservation evidence with other species
  4. A unique CCDS ID is assigned to the CCDS, which links together all of the gene annotations with the same CDS.
