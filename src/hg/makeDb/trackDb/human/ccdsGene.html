Description

The CCDS Genes track shows the $organism genome high-confidence gene annotations from the Consensus CDS (CCDS) project. This project is a collaborative effort to identify a core set of human protein coding regions that are consistently annotated and of high quality. The long term goal is to support convergence towards a standard set of gene annotations on the human genome.

Collaborators

National Center for Biotechnology Information (NCBI)
University of California, Santa Cruz (UCSC)

Methods

  1. CDS annotations of the $organism genome are obtained from two sources:
    • NCBI RefSeq
    • A union of the gene annotations from Ensembl and Vega Vega, collectively known as Hinxton.
  2. genes with identical CDS genomic coordinates in both sets become CCDS candidates
  3. genes undergo quality evaluation, which currently uses the following criteria:
    • have an initiating ATG, a valid stop-codon, and no in-frame stop-codons.
    • can be translated from the genome without frameshifts
    • have consensus splice-sites
    • don't intersect putative pseudogenes predictions
    • have supporting transcripts and protein homology
    • have conservation evidence with other species
  4. all collaborators must agree to pass the quality evaluation
  5. a unique CCDS id is assigned to the CCDS, which links together all of the gene annotations with the same CDS.
