Description

C/D box and H/ACA box small nucleolar RNAs (snoRNA) are non-coding RNAs that guide the 2'O-ribose methylation (C/D box) or the pseudouridilation (H/ACA box) of rRNAs and snRNAs. Contributed by Michel Weber at Laboratoire de Biologie Moléculaire Eucaryote, CNRS Université Paul Sabatier, Toulouse, France.

The miRNA data shows microRNAs from the miRNA Registry at the Wellcome Trust Sanger Institute. Mature miRNAs (miRs) are represented by thick lines; the predicted stem-loop portions of the primary transcripts are indicated by thinner lines. When a single precursor produces two mature miRs from its 5' and 3' parts, it is displayed twice with the two different positions of the mature miR.

The C/D box snoRNAs are shown in blue, the H/ACA box snoRNAs are shown in green and microRNAs are shown in red.

Methods

Mature and precursor miRNAs from the miRNA Registry are aligned against the genome using blat. The extents of the precursor sequences are not generally known, and are predicted based on base-paired hairpin structure. The miRNA Registry is described in Griffiths-Jones S (2004), The microRNA Registry, Nucl. Acids Res. 32:D109-D111.
