Description

This track shows putative binding loci of c-myc as determined by STAGE (Sequence Tag Analysis of Genomic Enrichment). The c-myc (cellular myelocytomatosis) protein is a transcription factor associated with cell proliferation, diffentiation, and neoplastic disease.

STAGE was performed in HeLa cells under normal growth conditions (10% Fetal Bovine Serum) using anti-myc antibody. Cloned STAGE tags were sequenced and mapped to the human genome as described in Kim et al Nature Methods 2005, referenced below.

The Tags subtrack shows all STAGE tags within the ENCODE region and thus represent the raw data. The Peaks subtrack shows high confidence c-myc binding regions derived from the STAGE tags.

Methods

Repeat masker was applied at low stringency (score more than 1500) to exclude tags within repeat regions.

Each tag was assigned a probability of enrichment that was calculated from the frequency of occurrence of the tag in the STAGE sequencing pool and the number of times the tag is present in the genome, assuming a binomial distribution. Tags having genomic frequency of more than 10 were discarded. In general, a low STAGE pool frequency with a high genomic frequency generates a score close to 1, indicating a very low probability of enrichment. For purposes of visualization, these tags were assigned an artificially inflated score of 300, however for analysis, a score of 1 should be used.

Peaks were determined by using a 500 bp window to scan across each chromosome. Each window was assigned a probability based on the tags mapped within that window.

Verification

Scores generated from the real data were cmopared to simulations where similar numbers of tags were randomly sampled from the genome. Calculating probabilities as above, a probability cut-off of 0.8 gave a false positive rate of less than 0.05.
