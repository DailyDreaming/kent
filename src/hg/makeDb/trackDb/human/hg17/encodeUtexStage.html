Description

This track shows putative binding loci of c-Myc and STAT1 as determined by Sequence Tag Analysis of Genomic Enrichment (STAGE). The c-Myc (cellular myelocytomatosis) protein is a transcription factor associated with cell proliferation, diffentiation, and neoplastic disease. STAT1 is a signal transducer and transcription factor that binds to IFN-gamma activating sequence.

STAGE was performed in HeLa cells under normal growth conditions (10% Fetal Bovine Serum) with anti-Myc, or in IFN-gamma stimulated cells with anti-STAT1 antibody. Cloned STAGE tags were sequenced and mapped to the human genome as described in Kim et al. (2005), referenced below.

The Tags subtrack shows all STAGE tags within the ENCODE region and thus represents the raw data. The Peaks subtrack shows high confidence c-Myc binding regions derived from the STAGE tags.

Methods

RepeatMasker was applied at low stringency (score more than 1500) to exclude tags within repeat regions. Each tag was assigned a probability of enrichment calculated from the frequency of occurrence of the tag in the STAGE sequencing pool and the number of times the tag is present in the genome, assuming a binomial distribution.

For the c-Myc data, tags having a genomic frequency of more than 10 were discarded. For the STAT1 data, a 500 bp window was used to scan across each chromosome. Each window was now assigned a probability based on the tags mapped within that window, and each tag was assigned a score calculated simply as (nocc/nhit)*1000. If the score for a tag exceeded 1000, it was reset to 1000.

In general, a low STAGE pool frequency with a high genomic frequency generates a score close to 1, indicating a very low probability of enrichment.

Peaks were determined by using a 500 bp window to scan across each chromosome. Each window was assigned a probability based on the tags mapped within that window.

Verification

Scores generated from the real data were compared to simulations where similar numbers of tags were randomly sampled from the genome. Calculating probabilities as above, for c-Myc data, a probability cut-off of 0.8 gave a false positive rate of less than 0.05, and for STAT1 data, a probability cut-off of 0.95 gave a false positive rate of less than 0.01. Additionally, 10 STAGE detected STAT1 binding sites were assayed by qPCR analysis and confirmed 9 out 10 as true positives, so the false positive rate is estimated at 10%.
