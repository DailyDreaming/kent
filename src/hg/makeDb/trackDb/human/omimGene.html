Description

This track shows a set of UCSC canonical genes that might be referenced by the Online Mendelian Inheritance in Man (OMIM) database, based on cross-reference information from RefSeq and UniProt.

OMIM is a compendium of human genes and genetic phenotypes. The full-text, referenced overviews in OMIM contain information on all known mendelian disorders and over 12,000 genes. OMIM is authored and edited at the McKusick-Nathans Institute of Genetic Medicine, Johns Hopkins University School of Medicine, under the direction of Dr. Ada Hamosh. This database was initiated in the early 1960s by Dr. Victor A. McKusick as a catalog of mendelian traits and disorders, entitled Mendelian Inheritance in Man (MIM).

NOTE: OMIM is intended for use primarily by physicians and other professionals concerned with genetic disorders, by genetics researchers, and by advanced students in science and medicine. While the OMIM database is open to the public, users seeking information about a personal medical or genetic condition are urged to consult with a qualified physician for diagnosis and for answers to personal questions.

Methods

This annotation was constructed as follows:

  • A set of UCSC genes was compiled that have cross-references to RefSeq genes with associated OMIM IDs, as defined in the Genome Browser database refLink table.
  • A second set of UCSC genes was compiled in which the corresponding UniProt proteins have external links to OMIM, based on data in the spXref2 table in the UCSC proteome database.
  • The two gene sets were combined and duplicates removed, resulting in a subset of UCSC genes that have associated OMIM IDs.
  • Each of the genes in the combined set was replaced with its associated canonical gene, based on data from the UCSC canonical gene set defined by the knownIsoforms and knownCanonical tables.
  • Duplicates were removed from the canonical set, and the data were sorted and loaded into the omimGene table.

In addition, data from the OMIM genemap, morbidmap files were loaded into the omimGeneMap, omimMorbidMap tables in the Genome Browser database. Descriptive information from this table is displayed on the OMIM Related Genes details pages. If an OMIM entry is listed in morbidmap, the entry is marked with red color.
