  Jobs with the UCSC Genome Project
  Software Developer

The UCSC Genome Bioinformatics group is currently looking for two Software Developers to join our browser development team.

Job responsibilities include:

  • Program development and implementation: programming CGI scripts in C; testing and debugging programs; working with a large Linux cluster and programming in a Linux environment.
  • Database utilization: interfacing with MySQL; designing new schema from MySQL; adjusting software to optimize database performance.
  • Hardware evaluation: analyzing and evaluating hardware performance; reworking software to take advantage of hardware capabilities.
  • Communication/Collaboration: interacting with scientific collaborators worldwide; understanding and/or learning details of the scientific goals of the Genome Project at UCSC.
Applicants should have the following qualifications:
  • BA or BS in Computer Science or equivalent experience; at least two years experience working in C in a Unix environment; strong communications skills, both written and verbal; knowledge of bioinformatics methods and applications or a strong desire to learn.
  • The abilities to: study and assimilate technical structure, data and requirements of genome analysis software; keep current with bioinformatics methods used at UCSC and elsewhere; quickly grasp new concepts and integrate into projects as appropriate; think on one's feet and work under pressure, with multiple deadlines; work, interact and communicate effectively with a wide range of people.
  • Preferred Qualifications: knowledge, background or experience in molecular biology and/or bioinformatics; extensive experience in other programming languages in addition to C.
To read detailed information about the position or to submit an application, see the UCSC Employment website.
