Software Quality Assurance Engineer:

The UCSC Center for Genomic Sciences, part of UCSC's Center for Biomolecular Science and Engineering (CBSE), is a multi-investigator organization focused on producing and maintaining updated assemblies of the human genome and genomes of numerous other species as both a downloadable database and an interactive, customizable web browser. The browser provides views of assembled genome sequences that are integrated with details of evolutionary conservation, synteny, genetic map markers, known and predicted genes, SNPs, transcript structure, alternative splicing, and a host of other critical data. In collaboration with faculty from the Department of Molecular, Cell, and Developmental Biology, the center fosters cross-disciplinary approaches to biological research. We currently seek a highly motivated quality assurance engineer to join our growing UCSC genome analysis group.

Summary of Duties:

Under the general supervision of the Software Quality Assurance Lead, the selected candidate will test software and verify data for the UCSC Genome Browser, a web-based tool created at UCSC and used by thousands of scientists and biomedical researchers worldwide.

Specific Responsibilities:

  • Manual software testing (30%): execute, enhance, and create software test protocols and procedures; participate in defect tracking and verification; troubleshoot problems and isolate possible root causes
  • Data validation (15%): execute and enhance data-filtering programs to validate genomic annotation data
  • Web site maintenance (15%): manage publication of software and data to the public web server; execute and enhance maintenance strategies
  • Test automation (15%): execute and enhance software test automation for web site and database
  • Communication (15%): communicate effectively about technical problems with UCSC team members and with external scientists and researchers
  • Customer service (10%): assist in monitoring the email communications received from scientists off-site to answer questions and troubleshoot problems
Qualifications (with or without accommodation):
  • BA or BS in Computer Science with a year of college-level biology coursework, or a BA or BS in Biology with significant software engineering experience, or equivalent experience
  • Experience working with C or Java
  • Experience working in a UNIX or Linux environment
  • Experience working with database software such as MySQL, SQL-Server, Oracle, Sybase, or Informix
  • Strong verbal communication skills
  • Ability to write quickly and clearly
  • Ability to work well in a team environment and to interact and communicate effectively with a wide range of people
Preferred Qualifications:
  • Experience working on a web site
  • Significant experience testing software in a commercial or professional environment
  • Experience implementing automated software testing tools
  • Knowledge of bioinformatics methods and applications or a strong desire to learn them

To review qualification requirements and detailed information about this position and to submit an application, see the UCSC Staff Employment website. .

Research Specialist (Biologist):

The Howard Hughes Medical Institute (HHMI) has an immediate opening for a biological research specialist under Professor David Haussler at the UCSC Center for Biomolecular Science & Engineering. This highly skilled research specialist will advance our understanding of the genome by taking computational discoveries to the next phase of laboratory experimentation & validation. Research project areas may include genome evolution, comparative genomics, alternative splicing, protein structure, & functional genomics. Responsibilities include:

  • Designing & executing wet laboratory experiments de novo to explore and verify predictions generated from computational genomic research
  • Documenting experimental protocols, interpreting results, & suggesting further steps in the exploration
  • Helping to communicate experimental results to the scientific community
