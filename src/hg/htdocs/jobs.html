  Jobs with the UCSC Genome Project
 

This page lists selected job openings with the UCSC Center for Biomolecular Science and Engineering (CBSE). For a complete list of open positions with the center, see the CBSE job listings.

  • Software Developer (#03-09-19)
  • Microarray Database and Software Developer (#03-09-20)
  • Research Specialist (Biologist)
Software Developer: Under the general supervision of the principal investigators and project manager, support and extend the UCSC Genome Browser, a web-based tool created at UCSC and used by thousands of scientists and biomedical researchers worldwide. Responsibilities include:
  • Program development and implementation: constructing annotation databases for different genomes using various existing bioinformatics programs and shell scripts; programming CGI scripts in C; testing and debugging programs; working with a large Linux cluster and programming in a Linux environment.
  • Database utilization: interfacing with MySQL; adjusting software to optimize database performance.
  • Hardware evaluation: analyzing and evaluating hardware performance; reworking software to take advantage of hardware capabilities.
  • Communication/Collaboration: interacting with scientific collaborators worldwide; understanding and/or learning details of the scientific goals of the Genome Project at UCSC.

To review qualification requirements and detailed information about this position, or to submit an application, see the UCSC Employment website.

Microarray Database and Software Developer: Under the general supervision of the principal investigators and project supervisor, support and extend the UCSC Splicing Microarray Database. Additionally, develop and maintain a set of web-based tools allowing researchers to archive, access, and analyze the results of their gene expression and alternative splicing experiments. Responsibilities include:
  • Program development and implementation: programming CGI scripts in C; testing and debugging programs; working with a large Linux cluster and programming in a Linux environment.
  • Communication/Collaboration: interacting with biologist end-users worldwide to understand their scientific goals and translate them into useful database functionalities; interacting with bioinformaticians to install, test, and maintain computational tools for gene expression analysis; learning and understanding details of the scientific goals of the Human GenomeProject at UCSC in the area of microarray analysis.
  • Database utilization: interfacing with MySQL; designing new schema from MySQL; adjusting software to optimize database performance.
  • Hardware evaluation: analyzing and evaluating hardware performance; reworking software to take advantage of hardware capabilities.

To review qualification requirements and detailed information about this position, or to submit an application, see the UCSC Employment website.

Research Specialist (Biologist): The Howard Hughes Medical Institute (HHMI) has an immediate opening for a biological research specialist under Professor David Haussler at the UCSC Center for Biomolecular Science & Engineering. This highly skilled research specialist will advance our understanding of the genome by taking computational discoveries to the next phase of laboratory experimentation & validation. Research project areas may include genome evolution, comparative genomics, alternative splicing, protein structure, & functional genomics. Responsibilities include:
  • Designing & executing wet laboratory experiments de novo to explore and verify predictions generated from computational genomic research
  • Documenting experimental protocols, interpreting results, & suggesting further steps in the exploration
  • Helping to communicate experimental results to the scientific community

To review qualification requirements and detailed information about this position, or to submit an application, see the CBSE job listing.
