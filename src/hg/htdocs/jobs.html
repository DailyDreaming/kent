Software Quality Assurance and User Support Technician
Posted 7/28/04

The UCSC Center for Genomic Sciences, part of UCSC's Center for Biomolecular Science and Engineering, operates one of the most important scientific websites in the 21st century, genome.ucsc.edu. More than 5000 scientists worldwide use the site every day to access the fruits of the human genome project. The site combines cutting edge research in genomics with solid software engineering and maintains a simple but flexible user interface. Keeping the site up to date is no small job, and one of the most important aspects of this job is quality assurance and user support. We currently seek two entry-level biologists to serve as Software Quality Assurance & User Support Technicians (Programmer/Analyst I).

Summary of Duties:

  • Study and learn the current web site: understand how biologists are using the genome browser now; recommend new features to help biologists
  • Test new versions of the software: run the software thoroughly with an eye to the needs of the biologists who will be using the site; apply an understanding of basic genomics to detect anomalies and catch annotation errors; follow up to ensure errors are resolved
  • Examine new genomics data for quality: read the text describing the data set, then select one sample element and systematically use all display options available for that element; report problems encountered and ensure that they are resolved
  • Support users: assist in monitoring email communications received from scientists off-site to answer questions and troubleshoot problems; research answers to user questions regarding underlying biological assumptions and computational methods in the genome software and databases; follow through to ensure that user questions are answered satisfactorily.
  • Report problems: communicate effectively about technical problems with UCSC team members and with external scientists and researchers; using the existing reporting system, develop accurate, complete, terse descriptions of problems; develop an awareness of existing problems; assess the importance of problems; ensure that high priority problems are resolved

Qualifications (with or without accommodation):

  • BA or BS in a biological science or an equivalent combination of education and experience
  • Broad knowledge in biology and deep knowledge of gene sequence organization, genome maintenance, and gene expression and regulation in eukaryotes
  • Solid web surfing skills
  • Strong writing skills
  • Strong verbal communication skills
  • Abilities to: tackle a large problem in a systematic manner; isolate problems by controlling conditions; communicate effectively and diplomatically with a wide range of people; work harmoniously in a fast-paced setting with a group of friendly but busy people; manage details and follow through; be productive and efficient

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Excellent scores on the GRE exam in Biology or in Biochemistry, Cell, and Molecular Biology
  • Experience and demonstrated skill in a customer service environment
  • Unix and programming skills
  • Knowledge of bioinformatics methods and applications

Apply online through UCSC Staff Human Resources (Job Number 04-07-31).

Research Technician
Posted 6/2/04

The Howard Hughes Medical Institute conducts scientific research in laboratories affiliated with leading academic centers throughout the United States. We have an immediate opening for an entry level research technician in the laboratory of Professor David Haussler at the University of California Santa Cruz.

Initially, the research focuses on two areas:

  1. Elucidating the function of approximately 500 ultra-conserved DNA sequence elements that show 100% identity between human, mouse, and rat genomic sequences for at least 200 contiguous base pairs
  2. Testing the function of new genes identified by computational searches for elements conserved in most mammals including chimpanzee, but which show significant changes in human.
These experiments involve various molecular biology techniques and also mammalian cell culture.

Summary of Responsibilities: Under general supervision, assist scientists and other technicians in the performance of laboratory and investigative procedures by performing a variety of routine experimental techniques in molecular and cell biology, such as PCR and cell culture. Collect, verify, record, and compile data and information in support of research.

Minimum Qualifications (with or without accommodation):

  • BS in biology, molecular biology, biochemistry, or a related field or equivalent in education and related laboratory experience.

Knowledge, Experience, and Abilities:

  • General experience in molecular biology techniques, such as plasmid DNA preparation and PCR, as well as the maintenance of bacterial and tissue cultures, with an understanding of the underlying scientific principles.
  • Abilities: draft routine reports; keep accurate records of work performed; learn complex tasks with general instruction and use experience to perform a variety of new techniques; work, interact, and communicate effectively with a wide range of people; work well both independently and in a team environment.
  • Actively seeks opportunities to increase skills and expertise.
  • Flexibility to occasionally work on a weekend day.

Physical Requirements: Occasionally lifts and/or carries objects weighing up to 20 pounds, stoops and bends, and reaches and grasps with arms and hands. Possible exposure to malodorous vapors, low dose radiation, contamination by toxic chemicals and acids and presence of carcinogenic substances. Long periods of standing and/or sitting.

HHMI offers an excellent salary and comprehensive benefits, including health, dental, vision, and retirement plan. EOE, M/F/D/V.

Research Technician / Laboratory Manager
Posted 6/2/04

The Howard Hughes Medical Institute conducts scientific research in laboratories affiliated with leading academic centers throughout the United States. We have an immediate opening for a research technician / laboratory manager in the laboratory of Professor David Haussler at the University of California Santa Cruz.

Initially, the research focuses on two areas:

  1. Elucidating the function of approximately 500 "ultra-conserved" DNA sequence elements that show 100% identity between human, mouse, and rat genomic sequences for at least 200 contiguous base pairs
  2. Testing the function of new genes identified by computational searches for elements conserved in most mammals including chimpanzee, but which show significant changes in human.
These experiments involve various molecular biology techniques and also mammalian cell culture.

Summary of Responsibilities: Under the general supervision of the Research Biologist, assist in the development and implementation of investigative procedures to accomplish research goals and independently perform scientific investigative procedures, actively contributing to the research results of the laboratory. Complex scientific procedures include DNA manipulation, PCR, RNA work, recombinant protein expression, purification, immunobiology, microscopy, and tissue culture. Supervise and perform technical and administrative tasks in the research biology laboratory, which is expected to grow to a team of 4-6. Oversee equipment maintenance, ensure adequate supplies for experimental needs, assist the research biologist in personnel management, manage lab safety programs to ensure compliance with all applicable regulation. Assist in planning for laboratory space and facilities; recommend changes; maintain inventory of laboratory furniture and equipment. Serve as liaison to HHMI and host institution administrative offices.

Minimum Qualifications (with or without accommodation):

  • Either a BS in biology, molecular biology, biochemistry, or a related field (or equivalent in education and experience) with three or more years of lab experience, or an advanced degree in an appropriate area of science with one or more years of related laboratory experience.
  • At least one year in a supervisory role.

Knowledge, Experience, and Abilities:

  • Operational knowledge of advanced laboratory equipment.
  • Experience: basic molecular biological techniques, such as DNA manipulation, PCR, RNA work, recombinant protein expression, purification, and immunobiology; tissue culture techniques, such as cell line maintenance, transfection, and isolation of stable clones (experience with mouse embryonic stem cells a plus); microarrays.
  • Abilities: to supervise others effectively; to initiate and compose communications for various purposes in an appropriate style; to organize a laboratory environment, coordinate ordering, maintain equipment, and manage a cell culture facility; to review goals, analyze processes necessary to achieve those goals, and develop techniques and systems required to meet those goals (often explores new areas and seeks opportunities to apply new knowledge to work); to work, interact, and communicate effectively with a wide range of people; to work well both independently and in a team environment; to gain in-depth understanding of organizational structure both within and outside the work unit, including the role of each position in the group and in other groups.

Physical Requirements: Occasionally lifts and/or carries objects weighing up to 20 pounds, stoops and bends, and reaches and grasps with arms and hands. Possible exposure to malodorous vapors, low dose radiation, contamination by toxic chemicals and acids and presence of carcinogenic substances. Long periods of standing and/or sitting.

HHMI offers an excellent salary and comprehensive benefits, including health, dental, vision, and retirement plan. EOE, M/F/D/V.
