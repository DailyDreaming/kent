  Introduction
 

The Sessions tool allows you to configure your browser with specific track combinations, including custom tracks, and save the configuration options. Multiple Sessions may be saved for future reference, for comparison of scenarios or for sharing with your colleagues. Saved Sessions persist for one year after the last access, unless deleted. Custom tracks persist for at least 48 hours after the last time they are viewed.

This feature may be accessed via the Sessions link in the top blue navigation bar in any assembly. To ensure privacy and security, you must login to the genomewiki site and create a username and password. The Sessions tool makes use of the built-in security of the wiki tool. [*** this could use some expanding ***]

Individual Sessions may be designated as either "shared" or "non-shared" to protect the privacy of confidential data. To avoid having a new shared Session from someone else override your existing Genome Browser settings, you are encouraged to open a new web-browser instance or to save existing settings in a Session before loading a new shared Session.

  A Simple Example
 

We have created an a few example Sessions to demonstrate the usefulness of this tool. We started by creating a user in genomewiki by the name of Example.

Example 1

This example shows the primate (chimp and rhesus) nets for chromosome 2 in the hg17 human assembly — the primate chromosome that fused in humans. Once we configured our browser view just so, we saved the Session so that we could share it. We named our Session hg17_chr2_primate.

There are several ways for you to view our saved example Session.

  • Open the Session tool and in the Session Management section, under the Load Settings header, enter our username: Example and session name: hg17_chr2_primate. Then press the submit button to load the Session. To view the Session in the Genome Browser, press the Genome Browser link in the top blue navigation bar.
  • If we had pressed the Email link after we created the Session, we would have sent you an email with the following contents and clickable link:

    Here is a UCSC browser session I'd like to share with you: http://genome.cse.ucsc.edu/cgi-bin/hgTracks?hgS_doOtherUser=submit&hgS_otherUserName=Example&hgS_otherUserSessionName=hg17_chr2_primate.

    You can click on that link to open the Session in your browser.
  • If we had saved the browser settings to a local file, we could simply provide the location of that file and you could load it into your browser and see the browser exactly as we did. Press here to see such a settings file. This method works best when the file is in a location that you can access from your own computer or network. In this case, you can copy this file and paste it into a file on your own machine then load it into the Sessions tool.
  • Since you do not have access to our file system, where this settings file resides, it will be easier for you to load it using a URL. To do this, open the Session tool and in the Session Management section, under the Load Settings header, enter the URL where this file is located: http://genome.ucsc.edu/goldenPath/help/session_example1.txt
    Then press the submit button to load the Session settings. To view the Session in the Genome Browser, press the Browser link in the Updated Session section.

  Lifespan of a Session
 

Once you save a Session for the first time, it will be available to you (and others if you share it) for one year. If you access the Session again during that 12 months, it will then be avaliable for one year after the access date. So, as long as you access your Session at least once a year, it will persist on the server until you delete it.

Note that if your Session contains tracks that expire (such as Custom Tracks, BLAT results, or Genome Graph tracks), those tracks will expire at their normal rate, and your Session will persist without those tracks.


  Sharing a Session
 

Sharing vs. Not Sharing

Confidential Data

If you do not choose to share your Session, your data and browser configuration will not be available to other users of the Genome Browser. Any confidential data or locations of interest that you are working with will be safe from viewing by others. [*** do we need to say something about the data living in the database in a not-so-encrypted format that WE could access if we were so inclined? ***]

However, if you choose to share your Session, it will be available to anyone who knows the username and session name, has access to the local file, or knows the URL of the Session settings.

Pointing Someone to Your Session

There are three ways to let others know about your saved Sessions.

  • Each previously saved named Session appears with hyper-links named Browser and Email. The Browser link takes you to the Genome Browser with that session loaded. The resulting Genome Browser page can be bookmarked in your web browser and/or shared with others. The Email link invokes your email tool with a message containing the Genome Browser link.
  • If you have saved your settings to a local file, you can send email to others with the file as an attachment and direct them to http://genome.ucsc.edu/cgi-bin/hgSession.
  • If you have saved your settings to a web server, you can send email to others with a link such as http://genome.ucsc.edu/cgi-bin/hgSession?hgS_doLoadUrl=submit&hgS_loadUrlName=U where U is the URL of your settings file, e.g. http://www.mysite.edu/~me/mySession.txt. In this type of link, you can replace "hgSession" with "hgTracks" in order to proceed directly to the Genome Browser.

  Displaying Custom Tracks in a Session
 

In addition to displaying standard UCSC tracks in your Session, you can also display Custom Tracks and and Genome Graph tracks. Before you create and save your Session, be sure to upload your Custom Track or Genome Graph track. Please note that Genome Graph tracks and Custom Tracks that are hosted on the UCSC server only have a lifespan of 48 hours. If you create a Session that includes a Custom Track or Genome Graph track that has expired, the Session link will not display properly. [*** how? ***]

  Deleting a Session
 

In the Session Management section, under the My Sessions header, press the delete button next to the Session name you would like to delete. This will permanantely delete all details of the Session from the UCSC server. Any saved links to that Session will no longer work.

Unlike most other browser preferences, the Sessions settings are not saved in your user cart. Therefore, when you choose to reset your cart, it will not delete your saved Sessions.
