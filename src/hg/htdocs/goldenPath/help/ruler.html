Description

The Base Position feature in the Genome Browser annotations track image shows the genomic coordinates of the displayed region and -- when zoomed-in to the base level -- the base composition of the sequence underlying the annotation tracks display. Click the arrow to the left of the base display to show the base complement.

In dense display mode, clicking anywhere on the genome coordinate line will zoom-in the display by threefold, centered on the coordinate of the click. To toggle the track between dense and full mode, click anywhere on the base composition line.

In full display mode, the track shows three frames of translation. Start codons (the first methionines) are displayed in green; stop codons are colored red and labeled with "*". Codons within or on the boundaries of gaps are colored black and labeled with an "X" to indicate an error (e.g. a truncated codon). All other codons are shown in alternating shades of gray to indicate codon boundaries. Codons are labeled by the amino acids they represent. To view the translation on the reverse strand, click the arrow to complement the base display.
