  Coloring gene predictions by codon
 

The Genome Browser's codon coloring feature allows users to quickly validate and compare gene predictions. To turn on codon coloring, select the genomic codons option from the Color track by codons pull-down menu. Any track in the Genes and Gene Prediction Tracks group can be colored by codon.

How are the genes/gene predictions colored?
Starting at the cdsStart of each prediction, each codon is colored and labeled until the cdsEnd (when zoomed-in closely enough). Those on the negative strand are labeled and colored accordingly. The genePred record is followed exactly, with no consideration given to any mRNA from which the gene/gene prediction is derived. Therefore, the codons of a RefSeq gene shown may not correspond to the NCBI record. Some mRNA tracks (mRNA, blastz mRNA, and xenoMrna) may be colored according to the mRNA itself.

Start codons (the first methionine) are indicated in green and stop codons are shown in red. All other codons are shown in the original track color, with codon boundaries indicated by alternating lighter and darker shades.

Codons are labeled by the amino acid they represent. Codons on the boundaries of a gap are labeled at both ends of the gap; the cyan color of the label text indicates a partial codon. Stop codons are labeled with '*'; errors are represented by 'X' (e.g. a truncated last codon).

When are the genes/gene predictions colored?
To view codon coloring, the option must be is turned on, the track must be in squish, pack, or full display mode, and the browser must be zoomed-in to a level at which there is at least one pixel per codon. The zoom-level requirement varies with the image width of the browser. At the default image width value of 620 pixels, the annotation tracks window must be zoomed-in to at least 1400 bp or less to view the codon coloring. To view the labeling, the tracks image must be zoomed to within 3 times the base level. A gene prediction with an invalid CDS (such as 0,0) will not be colored.
