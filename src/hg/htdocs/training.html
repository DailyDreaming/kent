UCSC Genome Browser Seminars
 

The UCSC Genome Bioinformatics Group has partnered with OpenHelix to provide training seminars and online materials for our software users. The information is targeted at individuals with a basic knowledge of genomic and biological concepts; no programming skills are needed. Unless otherwise noted, classes and seminars are open to the general public.

"Introduction to the UCSC Genome Browser" - Tufts University School of Medicine, Tuesday, 10 August. Two 3-hour sessions available at 1:00 p.m. and 6:00 p.m. This introductory tutorial will cover the topics needed to effectively use the Genome Browser tool set, including: basic functionality of Genome Browser searching and BLAT use, Table Browser use, creating and using Custom Tracks, and an introduction to the Gene Sorter.

For more information and registration, see the OpenHelix website.
