  Citing the UCSC Browser in a Journal Publication
  There are various ways to cite the UCSC Genome Browser for a journal publication. Most journals accept a cite to a stable web page (1). Be sure to include the assembly date relevant to your work -- neither the reviewer nor the reader can replicate your use of the genome browser or table browser without it.

Three articles (2, 3, 4) provide references for the Genome Browser, the BLAT alignment tool, and autoSQL/autoXML respectively. Their PubMed abstracts will contain links to the site. The freeze date must still be provided. Third party tracks should be credited according to their source. Work pertaining to the genome assembly should cite the Human Genome Consortium paper (6). Work pertaining to genome assemblies prior to the 22 Dec 2001 freeze should also cite the GigAssembler (5).
