Notes: 
1. If a space is required in a node label, enter it as a dash.

2. Underscores and anything following them are automatically stripped from node labels 
unless the preserve-underscores checkbox is checked.

3. Length-legend and length-values cannot be shown unless use-branch-lengths is also checked.

4. The branch lengths are expected substitutions per site, allowing for
multiple hits.  So a branch length of 0.5 means an average of one
substitutions every two nucleotide sites, but the percent id will be
less than 50% because some of those substitutions are obscured by
subsequent substitutions.  They're estimated from neutral sites,
sometimes fourfold degenerate sites in coding regions, or sometimes
"nonconserved" sites according to phastCons.  The numbers are significant
to two or 3 figures.
